![](../figures/news/cairne_header.png)

The THK-AI Research Cluster at TH Köln is featured in the [CAIRNE
October 2025
Newsletter](https://mailchi.mp/claire-ai/cairne-may-newsletter-12872733?e=ccac9606f8).
Recognized by the TH Köln Executive Board on January 31, 2024 for the
2024–2028 period, THK-AI serves as the university’s central hub for
artificial intelligence under the guiding principle “Shaping social
innovation with AI.” The cluster fosters collaboration across
associations, industry partners, faculty, and students.

Led by Professors [Thomas
Bartz-Beielstein](https://www.linkedin.com/in/thomas-bartz-beielstein-3157b541?lipi=urn%3Ali%3Apage%3Ad_flagship3_profile_view_base_contact_details%3B3T8qGvOxQhWWllip%2BYBjDw%3D%3D)
and [Anja
Richert](https://www.linkedin.com/in/anja-richert-62868b33?lipi=urn%3Ali%3Apage%3Ad_flagship3_profile_view_base_contact_details%3BEN%2Fr7v3eThWjH0ellsAI3g%3D%3D),
THK-AI advances research across machine learning, optimisation, NLP,
computer vision, robotics, HMI, AI ethics, and high-performance
computing. With 17 senior researchers, several postdocs, and more than
30 PhD students, the cluster contributes to impactful application areas
including healthcare, industry, energy, environment, education, and
public services. Highlighted activities include projects such as
ShapeFuture and THKIplus.

This recognition underscores THK-AI’s role in Europe’s trustworthy AI
ecosystem and its commitment to impactful, interdisciplinary AI research
and innovation.
